## 1. Togelius et al. Search-based Procedural Content Generation: A Taxomy and Survey
**Date:** 6.15.26
**Synthesis:** Togelius et al. establishes a comphrehensive taxomy for Search-based Procedural Content Generation (SBPCG) specifically focusing on games wherein it discusses why procedural content generation is devised (primarily to save memory ("unexpanded" content), development resources especially in the context of hardware improvements and player level of expected detail, the expanding of game mechanics and the augumenting of human imagination). It further goes on to categorize how the content is generated comparing the pros and cons of different generation concepts as well as how it is then represented within games(through weapons, levels, puzzles etc.). The quality/fitness of the content is then evaluated in a broader context as well as the broader impacts/concerns.
